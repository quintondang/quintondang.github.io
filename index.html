About

I spent a decade as a software engineer building products used by millions — design systems at Square, frontend infrastructure at Optimizely — and I've never stopped thinking like a product person. I've owned roadmaps, written PRDs, aligned cross-functional stakeholders, and shipped features that moved the needle on adoption and accessibility compliance.

What sets me apart as a TPM candidate is the depth I bring to technical conversations. I can read a pull request, reason about API contracts, and hold my own in architecture discussions — which means I can de-risk estimates, unblock engineers, and translate complexity into crisp business context for leadership, all without becoming a bottleneck.

I'm now channeling that engineering foundation into product strategy full-time: defining the "what" and "why," partnering with design and engineering to ship the "how," and building the kind of platform trust that scales teams.
